# ONITA WEBSITE STRUCTURE

## Conversion-Focused Website Architecture

### Version 1.0

---

# Website Goal

The primary purpose of the website is **not to explain AI.**

The purpose is to convince visitors that **Onita lets any business operate like an enterprise using an AI workforce we build and run for them.**

The website should immediately answer:

> **"How can Onita help my business get more done without hiring more people?"**

Every page should move visitors toward one primary conversion goal.

---

# Primary CTA

Onita is a **done-for-you / managed** service, not self-serve. Throughout the website, every CTA
should lead to:

> **Book a Demo**

Secondary CTA / For Enterprise

> **Talk to Sales**

There is **no self-serve free signup or login** on the marketing site — no "Start Free", "Log in",
"Sign up", or "free trial". Prospects book a demo and our team designs, builds, hosts, and runs
their AI workforce for them. Never compete with more than these two CTAs.

---

# Core Brand Message

> Your team just got bigger.

Supporting Message

> AI employees that work together, automate repetitive work, and help your business get more done without hiring more people. We design, build, host, and run them for you.

---

# Core Positioning

Do **NOT** position Onita as another AI chatbot, and do **NOT** position it as a self-serve app.

Position it as

> The AI Workforce Platform — designed, built, and run for your business.

It is a **done-for-you / managed** engagement: book a demo, and our team stands up your AI
workforce end to end. No free tier, no login, nothing for the customer to build themselves.

Not

> AI Assistant

Not

> AI Chatbot

Not

> AI Workspace

Instead

> AI Workforce

This positioning is unique and immediately memorable.

# SEO Structure

Every page should include:

* One clear H1 focused on the primary search intent.
* Logical H2/H3 hierarchy with descriptive headings.
* Descriptive title tags (50–60 characters) and meta descriptions (150–160 characters).
* Canonical URLs.
* Open Graph and X (Twitter) metadata.
* JSON-LD structured data where applicable (Organization, Product, FAQ, Article, Breadcrumb, SoftwareApplication).
* Optimized images with descriptive alt text.
* Internal links to related products, solutions, industries, and resources.
* Fast-loading, indexable content that avoids relying solely on client-side rendering.

Key keyword clusters:

* AI workforce platform
* AI agents for business
* AI agents for teams
* AI agents for small businesses
* managed AI workforce
* AI workforce for growing companies
* done-for-you AI automation
* AI work management software
* business process automation
* enterprise AI agents

---

# LLM & AI Agent Optimization

Design content to be easily interpreted by AI assistants and answer engines:

* Use clear, descriptive section headings.
* Define concepts explicitly (e.g., "What is an AI Workforce?").
* Include concise summaries at the beginning of long pages.
* Add comparison sections such as "Onita vs ClickUp", "Onita vs Notion", and "Onita vs Asana".
* Publish feature pages with structured definitions, benefits, and use cases.
* Create FAQ sections that answer natural-language questions.
* Use semantic HTML (`main`, `section`, `article`, `nav`, `aside`, `footer`) and accessible markup.
* Ensure navigation and core content are crawlable without requiring JavaScript execution.

---

# Accessibility & Responsive Design

The experience should meet WCAG 2.2 AA standards and be fully responsive.

Requirements include:

* Keyboard-accessible navigation and interactive elements.
* Visible focus states.
* Sufficient color contrast.
* ARIA labels where appropriate.
* Screen-reader-friendly form controls and navigation.
* Responsive layouts for mobile, tablet, laptop, and large desktop breakpoints.
* Touch-friendly controls with adequate spacing.
* Reduced-motion support for users who prefer minimal animation.
* Optimized typography with readable line lengths and scalable text.

---

# Conversion Strategy

Every page should guide visitors through a consistent decision journey:

1. Capture attention with a clear business outcome.
2. Demonstrate the problem with fragmented tools and growing operational costs.
3. Introduce Onita as the unified AI Workforce Platform.
4. Show proof through product capabilities, customer stories, and measurable ROI.
5. Reduce friction with the managed model, security details, FAQs, and implementation guidance.
6. End with a single, unmistakable action: **Book a Demo**, with **Talk to Sales** as the secondary path.

The overarching message should remain consistent across the site:

> **Scale your business by expanding your workforce with AI—not your payroll.**
